Thug Notes explanations for books that aren’t classics

Prompting Meta to explain a book series in the voice and dialect that Thug Notes uses in his literary explanation of books
LLMs
prompting
logic
Published

April 13, 2024

Thoughts

I curious to see if Meta would be able to recreate a script that would sound similar to to the Youtuber Thug Notes. I first chose Great Gatsby because I know Thug Notes has covered this book already and Meta would be able to see what the vocabulary he uses and the type of humorous tone he shows. I usually read some type of children book sires during the summer and last one I chose Percy Jackson and wanted a refresher to what happened in the books. Honestly not too bad of a let down by Meta with the way it showed the Youtuber’s voice. One critique which I had was that it used very similar wording for the summaries, which I would have liked to have seen a bit more variance in the speech used or more phrases he usually uses in the summary videos. I could have prompted Meta to do other books to see if it would capture a different tone. # Initial Prompt: can you make a story like the youtuber Thug notes # Response: You want a summary of a literary work, like SparkNotes, but with a dash of humor and swag, like Thug Notes! Let’s do this! Which literary work would you like me to summarize? Please respond with the title, and I’ll create a Thug Notes-style summary for you! Example: “The Great Gatsby” or “Romeo and Juliet” (Note: Please keep in mind that my responses will be family-friendly and respectful, while still incorporating the playful tone of Thug Notes.)

can you expalin great gatsby # Response: Thug Notes: The Great Gatsby The Lowdown Novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald (1925) Set in the 1920s in Long Island, New York Story revolves around Jay Gatsby and his obsession with Daisy Buchanan The Plot Narrator Nick Carraway moves to Long Island and becomes friends with his neighbor, Jay Gatsby Gatsby’s got a mysterious past and a penchant for throwing lavish parties Gatsby’s goal: win back his lost love, Daisy (who’s married to the wealthy and brutish Tom Buchanan) Things get complicated when Nick arranges a meeting between Gatsby and Daisy Affairs, lies, and deceit lead to a tragic ending The Characters Jay Gatsby (a.k.a. James Gatz): Self-made millionaire with a shady past Daisy Buchanan: Gatsby’s lost love, married to Tom Tom Buchanan: Wealthy, arrogant, and cheating husband Nick Carraway: Narrator, moral compass, and Gatsby’s friend Themes The American Dream: Gatsby’s pursuit of wealth and love Class and Social Status: Old money vs. new riches Love and Illusion: Gatsby’s all-consuming passion for Daisy The Tea Gatsby’s parties symbolize the excess and superficiality of the Roaring Twenties The green light across the water represents the elusive American Dream The novel critiques the corrupting influence of wealth and the decay of moral values The Verdict A classic tale of love, greed, and the dark underbelly of the American Dream A must-read for anyone interested in literature, history, or just a darn good story! How’s that? Did I do Thug Notes proud?
